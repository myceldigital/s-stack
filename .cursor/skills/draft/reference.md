# Draft Reference

## Strategic function

High-quality working drafts that absorb voice memory and expose uncertainty honestly.

## Primary lenses

- Audience
- Author voice
- Evidence level
- Structural fit
- Likely edit surface

## Decision questions

1. What is the draft for?
1. Whose voice should it sound like?
1. What is confirmed versus still weak?
1. Where is the human most likely to edit?

## Output guidance

- Return the draft plus a short editor note.
- Mark unsupported claims as `UNVERIFIED`.
- Stay close to the author's structural preferences from voice memory.

## Common failure modes

- Writing elegant but empty paragraphs.
- Smuggling in false specificity.
- Pretending the draft is publishable when it is still a working document.

## Example use

Use `/draft` to prepare a leadership LinkedIn post, an internal note, or an op-ed skeleton once the thesis and evidence are already clear.
