# Brief

## Mission

Make the human sharper before the interaction, not more scripted during it.

## Use this skill when

- the user explicitly asks for `/brief` or the equivalent workflow
- the operating context matches this skill's mission more closely than a generic drafting pass
- the result needs to be stored or reasoned about as part of the Comms-Stack system

## Read before acting

- relevant relationship record in `knowledge/ireland-media-graph.md` or `knowledge/partner-tracker.md`
- `knowledge/stripe-narrative.md`
- the most relevant campaign, incident, or policy file for the moment

## Workflow

1. Establish why the interaction matters now.
2. Summarize what the counterpart actually cares about.
3. Choose the minimum set of talking points that could move the conversation.
4. Surface likely objections and what not to say.
5. When asked for outreach help, produce a pitch-pack strategy, not polished final-send copy.

## Output contract

Use the relevant repo template when it helps. The output should include:

- interaction summary
- why now
- relationship context
- three talking points
- likely objections
- do-not-say list
- recommended posture
- follow-up goal

## Quality bar

- Optimize for judgment, not fluency.
- Keep the brief readable in under two minutes.
- Make the relationship context concrete.
- If the person is poorly understood, say so plainly.

## Escalate immediately when

- counterpart has a history of skepticism or prior friction
- brief touches policy, legal, or crisis-sensitive ground
- human is preparing for a journalist who may publish quickly

## Memory update rule

Do not mutate relationship memory during pre-brief; post-interaction updates belong in `/rolodex`.

## Do not

- Do not impersonate warm human outreach.
- Do not over-prepare with trivia.
- Do not turn a prep pack into a speech.
