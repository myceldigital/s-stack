# Brief Reference

## Strategic function

Pre-interaction preparation that sharpens judgment without scripting the human.

## Primary lenses

- Counterpart incentives
- Relationship history
- Timing
- Likely objections
- Desired follow-up

## Decision questions

1. Why does this interaction matter now?
1. What does the other party actually care about?
1. What should the operator absolutely avoid saying?
1. What would a good outcome look like by the end of the interaction?

## Output guidance

- One page is the default.
- Bias toward talking points and traps, not background essays.
- For pitch-pack mode, give strategy and angle notes rather than final-send prose.

## Common failure modes

- Over-briefing until the operator sounds rehearsed.
- Filling the brief with facts that do not affect the conversation.
- Using AI polish where human texture is strategically superior.

## Example use

Use `/brief` before a call with an Irish Times reporter who is skeptical of broad tech claims but receptive to founder-backed data stories.
