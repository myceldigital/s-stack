# Policy

## Mission

Give the comms lead enough policy intelligence to coordinate well without pretending to replace policy experts.

## Use this skill when

- the user explicitly asks for `/policy` or the equivalent workflow
- the operating context matches this skill's mission more closely than a generic drafting pass
- the result needs to be stored or reasoned about as part of the Comms-Stack system

## Read before acting

- `knowledge/policy-landscape.md`
- `knowledge/stripe-narrative.md`
- relevant campaign or war room context if the policy signal intersects active work

## Workflow

1. Classify the development as immediate, near-term, or structural.
2. Explain why it matters to communications rather than summarizing policy for its own sake.
3. Identify who needs to be looped in.
4. Draft talking points or implication notes with clear confidence levels.
5. Surface what remains uncertain.

## Output contract

Use the relevant repo template when it helps. The output should include:

- policy brief
- communications relevance
- stakeholder impact
- talking points
- coordination recommendation

## Quality bar

- Translate policy into comms implications.
- Avoid overclaiming legal interpretation.
- Respect uncertainty and process boundaries.

## Escalate immediately when

- development may trigger press attention
- matter is legally sensitive
- policy interpretation is contested
- topic changes the current narrative architecture

## Memory update rule

Update policy landscape only with durable developments or tracked milestones.

## Do not

- Do not cosplay as legal counsel.
- Do not flood the operator with low-value policy noise.
